# Realms of Valor

A fairy-tale, turn-based strategy game inspired by **Heroes of Might & Magic II**,
built from scratch in **TypeScript + HTML5 Canvas** with original pixel art.
Explore an adventure map with your hero, gather resources, build and recruit in
your castle, and fight tactical battles. The **RPG** version runs a **five-realm
campaign** that marches from a green valley through frozen wastes, a drowned
marsh and a volcanic hell to the Dark Lord's Obsidian Throne — your hero (army,
level and stats) carries forward from realm to realm.

> **Mobile-first.** The whole UI is responsive and touch-driven: it plays in
> **portrait or landscape** on a phone (no "rotate your device" — the canvas
> fills the viewport at the device's pixel density), and scales up to tablet and
> desktop. Drag to pan, tap to aim a route, tap again (or **March**) to move.

> All graphics are original pixel art drawn procedurally in a HOMM2-like
> storybook style. No copyrighted assets are used.

## Repository layout

This repo ships **two versions** of the game as self-contained apps that share
one origin when deployed:

- [`classic/`](classic/) — the original turn-based skirmish (the game described below).
- [`rpg/`](rpg/) — a hero-centric **RPG** campaign: five hand-built realms, each
  with its own land, foes and a four-quest chapter (quests, NPCs, dialogue).
- [`index.html`](index.html) — a small chooser page served at the site root.

## Running

Work inside whichever version you want — each is its own Vite project:

```bash
cd classic        # or: cd rpg
npm install
npm run dev        # start the dev server, then open the printed URL
```

Other scripts (run from inside `classic/` or `rpg/`):

```bash
npm run build      # type-check + production bundle into dist/
npm run preview    # serve the production build
npm run typecheck  # strict TypeScript check
npm run icons      # regenerate the PWA icon set into public/
```

## Install it (PWA)

The game is a **Progressive Web App**: build it, serve `dist/`, and your browser
will offer to **install** it as a standalone app. Once visited, a service worker
precaches the whole app shell so it **runs fully offline** — no connection needed
to play. New builds update silently on the next visit.

- **Manifest:** fullscreen, orientation-agnostic, with a storybook castle icon
  (regular and maskable variants in `public/`, drawn procedurally by
  `scripts/gen-icons.mjs`).
- **Service worker:** generated by [`vite-plugin-pwa`](https://vite-pwa-org.netlify.app/)
  (Workbox) in `autoUpdate` mode; registered from `src/pwa.ts`.

## Deploy (GitHub Pages)

Pushing to `main` builds **both** versions and publishes them to one **GitHub
Pages** site via [`.github/workflows/deploy.yml`](.github/workflows/deploy.yml):

- Chooser — `https://<user>.github.io/<repo>/`
- Classic — `https://<user>.github.io/<repo>/classic/`
- RPG — `https://<user>.github.io/<repo>/rpg/`

Each app uses a relative `base` (`./`), so it works under its subpath with no
extra configuration. Both run from the same origin, so the RPG build uses an
**isolated save key** (`VITE_SAVE_SUFFIX=-rpg`) — playing one version never
touches the other's progress — and a distinct installed-PWA name (`VITE_PWA_TAG`).

One-time setup: in the repository's **Settings → Pages**, set **Source** to
**GitHub Actions**. After that, every push to `main` deploys automatically (or
trigger it manually from the **Actions** tab via *Run workflow*).

## How to play

### Adventure map
- The **map fills the screen** and the camera follows your hero. **Drag** to pan
  and look around.
- **Tap a tile** to preview a route — a green dotted path shows what you can
  reach this turn; orange dots are beyond your remaining movement. **Tap the
  destination again**, or press the **▶ March** button, to set off. (Right-click
  or `Esc` cancels.)
- Walk over **resource piles** and **treasure chests** to collect them.
- Step onto a **mine** or **monster** to fight its guards; winning captures the
  mine (daily income) or clears the path.
- Step onto your **castle**, or tap **Castle** in the action bar when adjacent,
  to open the town screen.
- **End Turn** (button or `Enter`/`Space`) advances the day: you collect income
  from your town and captured mines, and every new week brings fresh recruits.
- Tap **Army** to slide up a sheet with your hero, army, movement, and a
  **minimap** with fog of war that clears as you explore.

### Castle (Knight town)
- Click a dimmed building plot to **build** it (one building per day). Prerequisites
  and costs are shown.
- Click a built **dwelling** to **recruit** creatures into your hero's army:
  Peasant → Archer → Pikeman → Swordsman → Cavalry → Paladin.
- The **Marketplace** lets you trade surplus resources for gold; the **Well**,
  **Statue**, and **Castle** boost growth and income.

### Battle (tactical)
- Stacks act in order of **speed**, shown in the **turn-order strip** under the
  top bar so you can plan around who moves next.
- On your turn, click a glowing tile to **move** or an enemy to **attack** (melee
  units must reach an adjacent tile — a dashed marker shows where they'll strike
  from; the **Archer** shoots at range while no enemy is adjacent).
- Hover an enemy to see a **damage forecast** — estimated damage, how many you
  may slay, and whether they'll **retaliate**.
- Melee attacks provoke one **retaliation** per round.
- **Terrain features** shape every field (hover any tile for a label):
  **boulders**, **pine trees** and **craters** are impassable cover that form
  lanes and chokepoints to screen your shooters; **marshes** are passable but
  cost extra movement; and **quicksand** and **witchfire** are hazards — end a
  move on them and the stack is wounded (sinks or burns), so route around them.
- **Wait** holds a stack to act later in the round (slower waiters go first);
  **Defend** raises its defense until its next turn; **Auto** resolves the rest
  instantly; **Flee** retreats (and loses the engagement). Keyboard: `W`/`D`/`A`/`F`.

### Goal
The **RPG** runs a five-realm campaign. Each realm is its own land with a
four-quest chapter — **meet** a guide, **hunt** a marked foe, **reach** a warded
site, then **storm the keep** in the northeast. Clearing a realm's chapter sends
you **Onward** to the next, carrying your hero (army, level and stats) and war
chest forward. The realms, in order:

1. **The Vale of Sunhaven** — green grassland, lakes and forest.
2. **Frostmere Reach** — frozen snowfields, a cracked ice lake and black pine.
3. **The Sunder Marsh** — a drowned fen of swamp, water channels and dead forest.
4. **The Emberwastes** — volcanic ashland, molten lava lakes and black rock.
5. **The Shadowmarch** — the Dark Lord's blighted realm and the Obsidian Throne.

Take the final Throne to win the campaign. If your hero's entire army is
destroyed, the quest is lost — start a **New Quest** to try again. (The
**classic** skirmish remains a single Dragon's Keep map.)

## Project structure

```
src/
  engine/    renderer (virtual resolution + camera), input, loop, scene manager, audio
  art/       palette + PixelSprite primitive; terrain, object, creature, town & UI sprites
  data/      resources, terrain, creatures, build trees, quests/NPCs/dialogue,
             the campaign (levels.ts) + mapgen + the five handcrafted realm maps
  game/      state, hero, army, pathfinding (A*), fog of war, economy, combat model, RNG
  scenes/    AdventureScene, TownScene, BattleScene
  ui/        HUD, side panel (with minimap), reusable widgets (panels/buttons/text)
  main.ts    bootstrap
```

## Design notes
- **Rendering:** a **responsive** virtual canvas whose coordinate space tracks
  the CSS viewport (so UI sizes stay physically consistent) while the backing
  store is scaled by the device pixel ratio for crisp text. Pixel art is drawn
  nearest-neighbor. Every visual is a baked offscreen canvas. Scenes lay out
  against the live `vw`/`vh`/`portrait` rather than fixed constants.
- **Combat** is "simplified tactical": cost-aware grid movement around terrain
  (boulders/trees/craters block, marshes/quicksand slow, quicksand/witchfire
  wound) + melee/ranged + one retaliation,
  a speed-based initiative order, **Wait** and **Defend** stances, and a
  non-random damage forecast, with HOMM2-style damage
  (`count × dmg × attack/defense multiplier`). No spells, morale, or luck.
- **Faction:** four playable towns (Castle, Rampart, Dungeon, Necropolis), each
  with its own six-tier lineup; the chosen faction's hero and castle carry across
  the whole campaign.
- **Campaign (RPG):** a `CAMPAIGN` of five `LevelDef`s ([`data/levels.ts`](rpg/src/data/levels.ts))
  binds each realm's map builder, enemy faction, four-quest chapter and
  intro/outro text. Realms are generated by a shared seeded `MapGrid`
  ([`data/mapgen.ts`](rpg/src/data/mapgen.ts)) — terrain painters (blob/ribbon/
  clusters/border) plus a reachability pass that carves a corridor to any
  objective a procedural wall would otherwise strand, so no chapter can
  soft-lock. Eleven terrain types (grass, dirt, sand, water, forest, mountain,
  rock, **snow, ice, swamp, lava, ash**) give each realm its own look and
  movement feel. Completing a chapter advances the campaign; completing the
  final realm wins it.
